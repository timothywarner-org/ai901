## 1. Purpose

This policy explains how Contoso full-time employees earn, request, and carry over **Paid Time Off
(PTO)**. PTO is a single pooled bank that covers vacation, personal days, and short illnesses.

## 2. Accrual rate

New hires accrue **15 PTO days per year**, which works out to **1.25 PTO days per month** of service.
Accrual begins on the employee's first day and posts on the last calendar day of each month.

Accrual increases with tenure:

| Years of service | Annual accrual | Monthly accrual |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 2 years (new hires) | 15 days | 1.25 days |
| 3 to 5 years | 20 days | 1.67 days |
| 6 or more years | 25 days | 2.08 days |

There is no accrual during unpaid leave of more than 30 consecutive days.

## 3. Requesting PTO

Employees request PTO in the Contoso HR portal at least **5 business days** in advance for any absence
of two or more days. A manager must approve the request before the dates are confirmed. Same-day
requests for illness are approved automatically and reconciled the next business day.

## 4. Carryover and cap

Employees may carry over up to **5 unused PTO days** into the next calendar year. Any balance above
the 5-day carryover cap is forfeited on December 31 unless the employee's manager has approved a
written exception. The maximum total PTO balance at any time is **30 days**; accrual pauses when an
employee reaches the cap and resumes once the balance drops below it.

## 5. Payout on separation

When an employee leaves Contoso, any unused accrued PTO up to the 30-day cap is paid out at the
employee's final base rate in the last paycheck.
